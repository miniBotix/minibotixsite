A circuit is a complete path through which electric current can flow. In simple words, it's like a loop made of wires and components that allows electricity to move around and power things.

A circuit connects different electronic components such as resistors, LEDs, capacitors, and batteries using wires or PCB tracks (copper lines on a board). If the path is complete (closed), the electricity flows. If the path is broken (open), it stops.

Circuits allow us to build everything from simple blinking lights to complex devices like mobile phones, robots, and computers.
