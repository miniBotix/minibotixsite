A PCB (Printed Circuit Board) is a flat board made of non-conductive material with layers of copper tracks. It acts as the backbone of all modern electronics‚Äîfrom simple LED projects to complex microcontrollers‚Äîproviding both physical mounting and electrical connectivity for components.

PCBs use conductive traces instead of loose wires, which reduces errors and makes designs reliable. They are essential in industries like aviation, consumer electronics, robotics, and IoT devices.

üìÇ Types of PCBs

Single-Sided: These have copper on only one side of the board. They‚Äôre ideal for low-density designs like power supply circuits, LED lights, and household electronics. Since they‚Äôre easy to manufacture, they‚Äôre also cost-effective for simple projects.

Double-Sided: Copper layers are available on both sides of the substrate. Vias (holes) are used to connect the two sides. Commonly used in audio amplifiers, industrial controls, and test instruments where medium complexity is needed.

Multi-Layer: These boards contain three or more copper layers stacked together, separated by insulation. These are used in complex applications like computers, smartphones, aerospace, and high-speed communication systems where size reduction and signal integrity are critical.

Rigid PCB: Made from solid substrate material (like FR4), they are strong and cannot be bent. Used in desktop PCs, medical equipment, and automotive control systems. Rigid PCBs ensure strong mounting and long-term durability.

Flexible PCB: Built on flexible substrates like polyimide, these can bend and fold. Perfect for wearables, mobile phones, medical sensors, and compact camera modules. Flexibility improves packaging and vibration resistance.

Rigid-Flex PCB: A hybrid of rigid and flexible PCBs, these are used in complex devices where both strength and flexibility are required, such as in aerospace, medical implants, and military systems.

üìê Layers & Copper Tracks

A PCB can have several layers, each serving a unique function:

  • Top Layer: Surface-mount components are placed here.
  • Inner Layers: Used in multi-layer boards for power and signal routing.
  • Bottom Layer: For routing and through-hole components.

Copper Tracks are like highways for electrical signals. They connect components and must be carefully designed based on current, signal speed, and spacing. Power planes and ground planes help reduce noise and improve stability.

üßæ Gerber Files Explained

Gerber files are the output of PCB design software containing precise instructions for PCB manufacturers: board layers, drill holes, solder masks, and silk screen. Each Gerber file describes a specific layer or mask in your PCB design.

Think of them like architectural blueprints. You zip these files and upload them to fabrication sites like JLCPCB or PCBWay. Without accurate Gerber files, your PCB cannot be fabricated correctly.

Typical Gerber files include:

  • Top & bottom copper layer
  • Solder mask & silkscreen
  • Board outline
  • Drill files
